<h2>Project Summary</h2>
The spotted lanternfly (SLF) is an invasive species that has been spreading across the U.S. and causing major damage to crops, especially grapes and fruit trees. One of the biggest challenges is how quickly they reproduce, since each egg mass can contain dozens of eggs and can be laid on almost any surface. Current removal methods are pretty inconsistent and don’t always ensure the eggs are fully destroyed, which makes it hard to actually slow the spread.

Our project focuses on targeting the problem at that stage by designing a device that can reliably remove and contain egg masses. Instead of using basic tools like cards or scrapers, we built a mechanical system that removes the eggs in a single motion while also collecting them, so they can’t continue to hatch. The prototype is designed to be lightweight, ergonomic, and usable at different heights, while also giving a more consistent and measurable way to manage egg removal. Overall, the goal is to make early stage SLF control more efficient, practical, and scalable.
